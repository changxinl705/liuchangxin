Decentralized Composite Optimization over Networks

Decentralized optimization methods have received increasing attention recently due to their key role in advancing future developments of many engineering areas as diverse as networked control systems, sensor networks, and machine learning. In this project, we consider the general finite-sum optimization problem setup, where each local objective is composed of a smooth function and a non-smooth regularization term, and we are particularly interested in developing efficient optimization algorithmic frameworks with theoretical convergence guarantees.

Related papers

  • Changxin Liu, Huiping Li and Yang Shi, Resource-aware exact decentralized optimization using event-triggered broadcasting. IEEE Transactions on Automatic Control, DOI: 10.1109/TAC.2020.3014316

Robust Model Predictive Control and Scheduling Co-design for Cyber-physical Systems

In modern cyber-physical systems (CPSs) where the control signals are generally transmitted via shared communication networks, there is a desire to balance the closed-loop control performance with the communication cost necessary to achieve it. In this context, aperiodic real-time scheduling of control tasks comes into being and has received increasing attention recently. It is well known that model predictive control (MPC) is currently widely utilized in industrial control systems and has greatly increased profits in comparison with the proportional-integral-derivative (PID) control. As communication and networks play more and more important roles in modern society, there is a great trend to upgrade and transform traditional industrial systems into CPSs, which naturally requires extending conventional MPC to communication-efficient MPC to save network resources. Motivated by this fact, this project is dedicated to developing robust MPC and scheduling co-design algorithms for networked CPSs affected by both parametric uncertainties and additive disturbances.

Related papers

  • Changxin Liu, Huiping Li, Yang Shi and Demin Xu, Co-design of event trigger and feedback policy in robust model predictive control. IEEE Transactions on Automatic Control, 65(1): 302-309, 2020.

  • Changxin Liu, Huiping Li, Jian Gao and Demin Xu, Robust self-triggered min-max model predictive control for discrete-time nonlinear systems. Automatica, 89: 333-339, 2018.
